<p><ul><li>Conventional Foodservice System</li><li>Centralized Food Service System</li><li>Ready-Prepared Foodservice System</li><li>Assembly Serve Foodservice System</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The conventional foodservice system involves preparing food in bulk and storing it until it is needed. Centralized food service system prepares and delivers pre-plated meals to various satellite locations. Ready-prepared foodservice system cooks and chills food in advance, then reheats and serves it when ordered. Assembly-serve foodservice system involves purchasing pre-prepared meals that are reheated and served as individual portions. These market types cater to different needs and preferences, offering convenience, efficiency, and quality food options across various foodservice establishments.</p></p>
