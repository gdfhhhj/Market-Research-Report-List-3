<p><p>Automotive Mineral Fluid Lubricants are categorized into three main types based on the composition of base oils used in their production. Paraffinic Lubricants are derived from paraffin wax, which offers excellent thermal stability. Naphtenic Lubricants are made from naphthenic base oils, known for their strong chemical resistance. Aromatic Lubricants contain high levels of aromatic hydrocarbons, providing good solvency and compatibility with various additives. Each type of lubricant offers unique benefits and is suitable for specific automotive applications.</p></p>
