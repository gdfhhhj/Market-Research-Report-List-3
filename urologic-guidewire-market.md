<p><p>Urologic guidewires are segmented into two main types: stainless steel coated guidewires and nitinol coated guidewires. Stainless steel coated guidewires are widely used in urologic procedures due to their high tensile strength and flexibility. Nitinol coated guidewires, made from a nickel-titanium alloy, offer superior flexibility and shape memory, allowing for precise navigation through complex urologic anatomy. These two types of guidewires play a crucial role in facilitating the insertion of various urologic instruments during procedures.</p></p>
