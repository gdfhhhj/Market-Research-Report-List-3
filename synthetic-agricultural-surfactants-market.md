<p><p>Synthetic agricultural surfactants are categorized into four main types based on their chemical composition: non-ionic, anionic, cationic, and amphoteric. Non-ionic surfactants are versatile and compatible with a wide range of pesticides. Anionic surfactants are effective at reducing surface tension and improving coverage. Cationic surfactants have a positive charge and are often used for specific applications such as herbicide formulations. Amphoteric surfactants can carry either a positive or negative charge depending on the pH of the solution, making them versatile for various agricultural applications.</p></p>
