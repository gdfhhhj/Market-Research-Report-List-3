<p><p>Wireless network cards can be categorized into two main market types: intrinsic and extrinsic. Intrinsic market refers to the cards that are built directly into the device, such as laptops or smartphones, offering convenience and seamless connectivity. On the other hand, extrinsic market includes standalone network cards that can be added to devices for enhanced wireless capabilities. While intrinsic cards offer integrated solutions, extrinsic cards provide flexibility and customization options for users looking to upgrade their wireless connectivity.</p></p>
