<p><p>Automotive antifreezes mainly come in two types: ethylene glycol coolant and propylene glycol coolant. Ethylene glycol coolant is the most common type used in vehicles and provides excellent protection against freezing temperatures. Propylene glycol coolant is less toxic and environmentally friendly, making it a popular choice for some consumers. There are also other types of antifreezes in the market, such as organic acid technology (OAT) coolants, hybrid organic acid technology (HOAT) coolants, and inorganic acid technology (IAT) coolants, each offering unique features and benefits for different vehicles and applications.</p></p>
