<p><p>Encrypted flash drives are available in two main market types: software-based and hardware-based. Software-based encrypted flash drives rely on encryption software installed on the drive to secure the data. This type of encryption is convenient and flexible, as it can be easily updated. On the other hand, hardware-based encrypted flash drives have encryption capabilities built directly into the drive, providing a higher level of security. These drives are typically more resistant to hacking or malware attacks.</p></p>
