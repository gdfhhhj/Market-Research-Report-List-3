<p><p>A polymer stent graft is a medical device used for treating aortic aneurysms. It is a tube-like structure made from a biocompatible polymer material that is inserted into the aorta to reinforce the weakened walls and prevent rupture. The stent graft provides a safer alternative to open surgical repair and offers various advantages such as minimally invasive procedure, reduced recovery time, and lower risk of complications.</p><p>The polymer stent graft market is witnessing significant growth due to the increasing prevalence of aortic aneurysms, along with advancements in minimally invasive procedures. Moreover, the rising geriatric population and the growing preference for endovascular surgeries are also contributing to the market growth. Additionally, the benefits associated with polymer stent grafts, such as decreased hospital stays and lower post-operative complications, are further propelling market expansion.</p><p>The market is also witnessing several trends that are shaping its growth trajectory. One prominent trend is the development of bioresorbable polymer stent grafts that gradually dissolve within the body after fulfilling their purpose. These stent grafts eliminate the need for long-term implantation and the risks associated with permanent implants.</p><p>Furthermore, technological advancements such as the integration of imaging modalities into stent grafts for real-time visualization during procedures are gaining traction.
