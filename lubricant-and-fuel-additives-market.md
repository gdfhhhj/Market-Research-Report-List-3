<p><ul><li>Single Component</li><li>Additive Package</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Lubricant and fuel additives can be categorized into two main types: single component additives and additive package additives. Single component additives are individual chemicals that are added to lubricants or fuels to improve specific properties, such as reducing friction or preventing corrosion. On the other hand, additive package additives are blends of multiple chemicals that work together to enhance various performance characteristics of lubricants or fuels, such as improving viscosity, oxidation resistance, and cleanliness. Both types play a crucial role in optimizing the performance and longevity of lubricants and fuels.</p></p>
