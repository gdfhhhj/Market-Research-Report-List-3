<p><p>Automotive active seat headrests come in two main types: headrest activation and seat back activation. Headrest activation refers to a system where the headrest automatically adjusts its position based on various factors such as the driver's height or seating position. On the other hand, seat back activation involves the entire seat back moving to provide support and reduce the risk of whiplash in the event of a rear-end collision. Both types of active seat headrests aim to enhance safety and comfort for vehicle occupants.</p></p>
