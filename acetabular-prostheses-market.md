<p><p>Acetabular prostheses are medical devices used in hip replacement surgeries. They are designed to replace a damaged or diseased acetabulum (the socket part of the hip joint) with an artificial socket made of metal or ceramic. This prosthesis is combined with a femoral component to form a complete artificial hip joint.</p><p>The market for acetabular prostheses has been experiencing steady growth in recent years. The increasing prevalence of hip disorders such as osteoarthritis, rheumatoid arthritis, and hip fractures has been a significant driver of market growth. Additionally, the rising geriatric population, which is more prone to hip problems, is also contributing to the increased demand for acetabular prostheses.</p><p>Technological advancements in implant materials and designs have also played a crucial role in market growth. The development of improved bearing surfaces, such as ceramic-on-ceramic and metal-on-metal, has led to enhanced implant durability and reduced wear rates. Furthermore, the introduction of minimally invasive surgical techniques has increased patient acceptance and improved surgical outcomes, thereby driving market growth.</p><p>The increased focus on product customization and patient-specific implants is another emerging trend in the acetabular prostheses market. <p><p>Acetabular prostheses are used in hip replacement surgeries to replace the damaged hip joint. There are two types of prostheses available in the market - cementless and cemented. Cementless prostheses are made of porous material that encourages bone growth and integration, providing long-term stability without the use of cement. On the other hand, cemented prostheses utilize bone cement to attach the artificial joint to the existing bone. Both types of prostheses have their advantages and the choice between them depends on the patient's condition and surgeon's preference.</p></p>
