<p><p>High temperature cameras are available in two main types of sensors: CCD and CMOS. CCD sensors offer high sensitivity and low noise, making them ideal for capturing high-quality images in extreme temperatures. In contrast, CMOS sensors are known for their lower power consumption and faster readout speeds, making them suitable for real-time imaging in high temperature environments. Depending on the specific needs of the application, users can choose between these two sensor types to ensure optimal performance in challenging conditions.</p></p>
