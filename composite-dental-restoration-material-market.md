<p><p>Resin-based composites are tooth-colored materials made of a mixture of resin and fillers, often used for aesthetic dental restorations. They provide good durability and aesthetic results. Poly-acid modified composites, on the other hand, contain an acidic component that helps improve their bonding to the tooth structure. These materials are known for their fluoride release properties, making them suitable for restorations in high-caries risk patients. Both resin-based and poly-acid modified composites are commonly used in dental practice for restoring decayed or damaged teeth.</p></p>
