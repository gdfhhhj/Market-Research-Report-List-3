<p><p>Liquid propellants are classified into three main types: petroleum-based, cryogenic, and hypergolic propellants. Petroleum-based propellants are commonly used in space launch vehicles and military rockets due to their high energy content and ease of handling. Cryogenic propellants, such as liquid hydrogen and liquid oxygen, are used in space exploration due to their high performance and efficiency. Hypergolic propellants are chemicals that ignite upon contact with each other, offering a simple and reliable propulsion solution for satellite maneuvering and spacecraft propulsion.</p></p>
