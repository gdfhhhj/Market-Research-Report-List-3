<p><strong>The Autostereoscopic Outdoor Displays Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Parallax Barrier</li><li>Integral Photography and Lenticular Arrays</li><li>Other</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Autostereoscopic outdoor displays are a type of technology that allows viewers to perceive three-dimensional images without the need for special glasses. They can be classified into three main market types: </p><p>1. Parallax Barrier: This technology uses a barrier layer, consisting of small vertical slits, placed in front of the display. The barrier ensures that each eye sees a different set of pixels, creating a 3D effect. </p><p>2. Integral Photography: This technique involves capturing multiple viewpoints of a scene using an array of lenses. These viewpoints are then combined to create a spatially accurate 3D image. </p><p>3. Lenticular Arrays: It uses an array of lenticular lenses placed on top of the display panel. These lenses direct different views to the left and right eyes, producing a 3D perception. </p><p>Apart from these market types, there may be other autostereoscopic outdoor display technologies in the market, each employing unique methods to deliver captivating three-dimensional visuals to viewers.</p></p>
