<p><p>Solid-state lighting refers to the use of semiconductor light-emitting diodes (LEDs) for energy-efficient lighting solutions. HID (high-intensity discharge) lighting includes mercury vapor, metal halide, and high-pressure sodium lamps. Plasma and induction lighting use gases and electromagnetic fields to produce light. Fluorescent lighting consists of tubes filled with mercury vapor and phosphor coating. The "others" category includes halogen lamps, incandescent bulbs, and compact fluorescent lamps. These different types of energy-efficient lighting options offer varying levels of efficiency, longevity, and cost-effectiveness.</p></p>
