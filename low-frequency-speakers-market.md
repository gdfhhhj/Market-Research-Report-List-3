<p><p>Low-frequency speakers market types include woofers, midwoofers, and subwoofers. Woofers are larger speakers designed to reproduce deep bass frequencies, typically below 500Hz. Midwoofers are slightly smaller and handle midrange frequencies between 500Hz and 2000Hz. Subwoofers are specialized speakers dedicated solely to reproducing extremely low frequencies, usually below 120Hz. Each type of speaker serves a specific purpose in a sound system, with woofers providing the foundation of the bass, midwoofers handling vocals and instruments, and subwoofers delivering intense low-end impact.</p></p>
